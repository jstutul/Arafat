Overview

Expora is a comprehensive property management software designed to simplify the way Bproperty handles its diverse range of property services. It serves as a unified platform for managing everything from property listings to client interactions. With Expora, Bproperty's 500+ employees can streamline workflows, collaborate more effectively, and provide clients with a seamless experience in buying, selling, and renting properties. This user-friendly solution revolutionizes property management, setting new standards for efficiency in the prop-tech industry.

Problem

Bproperty faced challenges managing its diverse property services. Using multiple tools led to confusion and inefficiency among its 500 employees. They needed a unified solution to streamline workflows and provide clients with better service. Expora was born to solve these problems, making property management simpler and more effective for Bproperty and its clients.

Solution

Expora bring them all together in one place. Expora is the comprehensive solution designed to unify Bproperty's property management processes. With intuitive interfaces, seamless integration, and powerful features, Expora streamlines workflows, enhances team collaboration, and delivers exceptional client experiences, setting new standards in prop-tech efficiency.

Sketches & Wireframes

In the inception phase of crafting the Online Test product, the dynamic collaboration between the product team and designers takes shape through sketching and wireframing. The process begins with free-flowing sketches, capturing raw ideas and layout possibilities. These sketches, guided by the Product Requirement Document (PRD), then evolve into structured wireframes. Wireframes act as the skeletal framework, meticulously outlining the product's structure and functionality. This planning ensures a solid foundation before delving into the design details, setting the stage for a user-centric and visually compelling Online Test platform.

Accessibility

Accessibility ensures that everyone, regardless of disabilities or impairments, can access and use digital products, services, or environments. It involves designing and developing with inclusivity in mind, considering factors like screen readers, keyboard navigation, color contrast, and alternative text for images. By prioritizing accessibility, we create experiences that are usable by all, promoting equality, diversity, and user satisfaction.

Bdjobs website is designed to meet AA Accessibility Standards to ensure that a large number of users can access it. Additionally, responsive design is an important aspect of the bdjobs website's design.

High Fidelity Design

High fidelity design refers to the detailed, polished version of a user interface or product design. It incorporates precise visual elements, such as colors, typography, imagery, and interactive components, to closely resemble the final product. High fidelity designs are used for testing and validation purposes, providing a realistic representation of the user experience before development.

1. Test Planning:

  • Collaborate with product analysts to outline comprehensive test plans, focusing on key functionalities and user interactions within the Online Test platform.

2. Test Execution:

  • QC professionals rigorously execute the test plans, navigating through the product to identify any discrepancies, glitches, or areas of improvement.

3. User Scenario Replication:

  • Simulate real-world scenarios to replicate user interactions, ensuring that the Online Test product performs seamlessly under various usage conditions.

4. Usability Assessment:

  • Assess the product's user-friendliness, seeking feedback on navigation, clarity of instructions, and overall ease of use from the user perspective.

5. Bug Identification and Tracking:

  • QC teams meticulously identify and document any bugs or issues discovered during testing, collaborating closely with development teams to address and resolve them promptly.

6. Performance Evaluation:

  • Evaluate the performance of the Online Test product under different load conditions, ensuring optimal responsiveness and stability.

7. Feedback Integration:

  • Leverage insights from product analysts and real users to refine the product, continuously iterating and enhancing features based on user feedback.

8. Comprehensive Reporting:

  • Provide detailed reports summarizing the testing process, including identified issues, resolved bugs, and recommendations for further improvement.

Measuring Success

To measure the success of the Online Test product, we employ a multifaceted measurement strategy encompassing various key performance indicators (KPIs) and user-centric benchmarks. Our approach involves:

1. User Satisfaction Surveys:

  • Regularly conduct user satisfaction surveys to gather direct feedback on the Online Test product's usability, features, and overall experience. Quantify satisfaction levels through a numerical rating system.

2. Completion Rates:

  • Analyze the percentage of completed assessments relative to initiated ones. Higher completion rates signify user engagement and a seamless assessment experience.

3. Time-to-Hire Reduction:

  • Measure the average time taken from the initiation of the recruitment process to the final hiring decision. A reduction in time-to-hire demonstrates increased efficiency facilitated by the Online Test product.

4. Candidate Success Metrics:

  • Evaluate the success rates of candidates who underwent online assessments. Track their progress within the organization, linking assessment performance to on-the-job success.

5. Error Rates and Bug Reports:

  • Monitor the frequency of errors and bug reports identified during QC testing and user feedback. A decrease in error rates reflects the product's stability and reliability.

6. Cost Savings Analysis:

  • Conduct a cost-benefit analysis by comparing the expenses associated with the Online Test product implementation against the savings achieved through streamlined recruitment processes and reduced time-to-hire.

7. Integration Success:

  • Assess the seamless integration of the Online Test product with existing recruitment and HR systems. A high success rate indicates compatibility and efficient collaboration.

8. Diversity and Inclusion Impact:

  • Track changes in the diversity and inclusion landscape by examining the demographic makeup of successfully hired candidates. An increase in diverse hires aligns with organizational goals.

9. Security and Compliance Adherence:

  • Regularly audit the security measures in place and assess compliance with data protection standards. High adherence indicates the robustness of the product in safeguarding sensitive information.

10. User Testing Feedback Incorporation:

  • Evaluate the effectiveness of incorporating user testing feedback into product updates. A high implementation rate demonstrates agility in addressing user needs and concerns.

Feedback & Design Iterations

Feedback & design iteration plays a pivotal role in refining and optimizing the user experience. This continuous improvement cycle is instrumental in ensuring that the product aligns precisely with user needs and industry standards.

1. Initial Design Implementation:

  • Begin with the implementation of the initial design based on the wireframes and user requirements outlined in the Product Requirement Document (PRD).

2. User Testing and Feedback Collection:

  • Conduct user testing sessions to gather valuable feedback on the initial design. Solicit input on usability, clarity of instructions, and overall satisfaction with the assessment experience.

3. Analysis of User Feedback:

  • Analyze the collected user feedback, identifying areas for improvement, potential pain points, and aspects that resonate positively with users.

4. Prioritization of Design Enhancements:

  • Prioritize design enhancements based on the severity and frequency of user feedback. Focus on addressing critical issues and enhancing features that significantly impact the user journey.

5. A/B Testing for Key Elements:

  • Implement A/B testing for specific design elements, such as question formats or navigation paths. This data-driven approach helps assess the performance and user preference for different design variations.

6. Documentation of Design Changes:

  • Maintain comprehensive documentation detailing each design iteration, including the rationale behind changes, user feedback incorporated, and the impact observed during testing.

7. Version Release and Monitoring:

  • Release updated versions of the Online Test product, closely monitoring user interactions and system performance post-iteration. Gather post-release feedback to identify any unforeseen issues and further areas for improvement.
